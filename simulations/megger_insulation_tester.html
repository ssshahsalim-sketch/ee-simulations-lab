What PI & DAR Tell You
PI (Polarization Index): Ratio of 10-min to 1-min IR. Shows if insulation is absorbing charge (good) or leaking (contaminated/wet).

DAR: Quick test — ratio of 1-min to 30-sec readings. Useful for field screening when full PI test is not possible.

Key: High PI = dry, clean insulation. Low PI = moisture, carbon tracking, or physical damage.
Recommended Action
Calculate PI/DAR first.

Complete Testing Standards Reference
IEEE 43-2013 — Motor/Generator IR
Minimum IR (MΩ) = kV rating + 1
(e.g. 6.6kV motor: min 7.7 MΩ)

At operating temp, minimum recommended:
• Stator windings ≥ 100 MΩ (machines >1kV, new)
• In-service acceptable: ≥ 5 MΩ
• PI required: ≥ 2.0 for all machines >1kV
• Test at rated voltage × 2 + 1000V (Hi-pot)
IEC 60076 — Transformer IR
HV Winding to ground + LV:
• <72.5kV: min 1000 MΩ at 2500V
• 72.5–170kV: min 3000 MΩ at 5000V
• >170kV: min 10000 MΩ at 10kV

Temperature correction critical:
IR doubles every 10°C drop in temp.
PI ≥ 1.5 (acceptance), ≥ 2.0 preferred.
IEC 60502 — Cable IR
LV Cable (0.6/1kV): ≥ 50 MΩ (new installed)
MV Cable (3.6/6kV): ≥ 500 MΩ
MV Cable (12/20kV): ≥ 5000 MΩ
HV Cable (36/60kV): ≥ 10000 MΩ

In-service aged cables: 1 MΩ/km minimum.
Note: Length reduces IR (parallel paths).
IR per km = measured × length(km).
IS 3043 / IEEE 80 — Grounding
IS 3043 (India) requirements:
• Residential: ≤ 10 Ω (1-ph), ≤ 5 Ω (3-ph)
• Industrial: ≤ 1–2 Ω
• HV Station: ≤ 1 Ω
• EHV Station: ≤ 0.5 Ω

IEEE 80 safety criteria:
Touch voltage ≤ 50V (wet), ≤ 25V (special)
Step voltage limits calculated per body weight.
Annual re-testing mandated by standards.
IEC 60364 — LV Installation
New LV installations (before commissioning):
• Between live conductors: ≥ 1 MΩ
• Between live and earth: ≥ 1 MΩ
• SELV/PELV circuits: ≥ 0.5 MΩ
• Special locations (bathroom): ≥ 0.5 MΩ

Test voltage: 500V DC (circuits ≤500V AC)
Test voltage: 1000V DC (circuits 500–1000V AC)
Safety Precautions (Critical)
⚠ ALWAYS isolate and lock-out before testing
⚠ Discharge cables/caps before and after
⚠ Never touch test leads during HV megger test
⚠ Ground all phases before test (except test phase)
⚠ Wait 4× test duration for discharge after test
⚠ Remove surge arresters before HV test
⚠ Disconnect sensitive electronics (VFD, meters)
✓ Use 3-wire test for accurate long cable tests
✓ Record temperature at time of measurement
Megger Test Procedure — IR
1. Isolate equipment (LOTO)
2. Disconnect all connections to equipment
3. Ground all non-test terminals
4. Connect megger: Line terminal → line, Earth → frame
5. Select correct test voltage (see chart)
6. Apply voltage for 60 seconds (1 min) minimum
7. Record reading at exactly 1 min
8. For PI: also record at 10 min
9. Press DISCHARGE before disconnecting
10. Record: date, temp, humidity, test voltage, IR value

Three-wire test: Connect guard terminal to remove surface leakage — essential for accurate readings on long cables or wet equipment.
Test Voltage Selection Guide
Rated voltage → Test voltage:
≤100V → 250V DC
100–500V → 500V DC
500V–1kV → 1000V DC
1kV–6.6kV → 2500V DC
6.6kV–33kV → 5000V DC
>33kV → 10000V DC

Never exceed: 2 × rated voltage for Hi-pot test on aged equipment. Use step-voltage test for suspect insulation.
Earth Test Methods
Fall of Potential (3-point):
Most accurate. Current electrode 10× depth away, potential electrode at 62% distance. Measures true earth resistance independent of contact resistance.

Clamp-on Earth Tester:
Non-intrusive, measures while energised. Best for grid/multiple electrode systems. Cannot measure single isolated rods.

Stakeless Method:
Two clamps around conductor, no probes needed. Quick screening tool.

Selective Method:
4-wire (Wenner) for soil resistivity mapping before installing earth grid.
Common Causes of Low IR
🔴 Moisture ingress / condensation
🔴 Carbon tracking from partial discharge
🔴 Physical damage to insulation
🔴 Contamination (oil, dust, chemicals)
🔴 Overheating / thermal aging
🔴 Incorrect cable termination
🔴 Galvanic corrosion at joint
🔴 Rodent damage (biotic attack)
🔴 UV degradation (outdoor cables)

Remedial actions:
• Dry out with space heaters (motors)
• Re-terminate cable ends
• Replace section with PD tracking
• Apply conformal coating (control wiring)
Insulation Classes & Max Temp
Class A: 105°C (cotton, paper, varnish)
Class B: 130°C (mica, glass fibre)
Class F: 155°C (most modern motors)
Class H: 180°C (silicone, mica)
Class C: >180°C (ceramics, PTFE)

Aging rule: Every 10°C above rated temp halves insulation life (Arrhenius law). A motor running 10°C hot at 155°C instead of 145°C has only half the expected lifespan.

Testing schedule:
New install: before commissioning
Routine: annually or per outage
Post-fault: immediately after tripping
Predictive: quarterly trend analysis
Trending & Condition Assessment
Keep historical records of all IR tests.
A sudden drop of >50% from last reading is alarming even if still above minimum.

Green flag: Rising or stable trend
Yellow flag: Gradual decline, plan maintenance
Red flag: Rapid decline, immediate action

IEEE 43 recommends plotting IR values on semi-log paper to identify trends. Temperature must always be corrected to 40°C for comparison across measurements.

Humidity Effect on IR
Humidity Correction
High humidity (>75%) can reduce IR readings by 50-90%. Always record humidity and prefer testing in dry conditions. IR rises rapidly when equipment is dried.
Temp Correction Chart
Temperature Correction Factor
IR doubles for every 10°C decrease in temperature. All readings should be normalized to 40°C for comparison.
