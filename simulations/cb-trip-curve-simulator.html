CURVE REFERENCE GUIDE

How to Read Trip Curves

X-AXIS — MULTIPLE OF RATED CURRENT (×In)

The horizontal axis shows the fault current as a multiple of the breaker's rated current (In). A value of 1× means the circuit is carrying exactly its rated current. Values above 1× indicate overload or fault conditions.

Y-AXIS — TRIPPING TIME (seconds)

The vertical axis shows how long before the breaker trips. Both axes use a logarithmic scale. The shaded band between two curves for each type represents the manufacturing tolerance — the breaker will trip somewhere within this band.

REGIONS EXPLAINED

No-Trip Zone: Below 1×In — breaker stays closed.  Thermal Zone: 1–5×In — bimetal strip bends and trips slowly.  Magnetic Zone: Above instantaneous threshold — breaker trips almost instantly (< 0.1s).
