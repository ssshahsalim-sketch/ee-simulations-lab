What Does "80% Rated" Mean?
NEC 240.20(A): Where a branch circuit supplies continuous loads or any combination of continuous and noncontinuous loads, the rating of the overcurrent device shall not be less than the noncontinuous load + 125% of the continuous load.

A standard (80%-rated) breaker is not limited to only 80% of its rating physically — all breakers are manufactured to carry 100% current indefinitely. The 80% is an NEC application restriction: when supplying continuous loads (running at full current ≥3 hours), you must size the breaker at 125% of load, meaning you only use 80% of its capacity for continuous load.

Example: 80A continuous load → 80 × 1.25 = 100A breaker required

What Does "100% Rated" Mean?
NEC Exception: Where the assembly, including the overcurrent devices protecting the branch circuit(s), is listed for operation at 100% of its rating, the ampere rating shall be permitted to be not less than the sum of continuous + noncontinuous loads.

A 100%-rated breaker has undergone additional UL 489 testing inside a minimum-size enclosure at rated current until temperature stabilization — verifying it won't trip and won't exceed temperature rise limits. The manufacturer then marks it with:

  • Minimum enclosure size (H × W × D)
  • Wire temperature rating required (75°C or 90°C)
  • Ventilation requirements (if any)

Same example: 80A continuous load → 80A breaker allowed

Checklist for 100% Rated CB Installation
☐ Verify listing: Confirm CB is UL listed as 100% rated
☐ Enclosure size: Use minimum enclosure per manufacturer marking
☐ Wire rating: Use 90°C insulated wire if required by marking
☐ Wire ampacity: Size conductors at 75°C ampacity even with 90°C wire
☐ Ventilation: Provide ventilation if required per marking
☐ Full system: Entire assembly must be listed for 100% operation
☐ AHJ approval: Check with Authority Having Jurisdiction
☐ Documentation: Keep manufacturer installation requirements on file
